## Part I: Design Logic

Most crypto analysis fails in one of two ways: too macro or too micro. Both fail in isolation. The 6-level framework forces a top-down read. This scoring system converts that framework from narrative into a repeatable numeric signal.

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Investment Posture |
|-------|-------|--------------------|
| 5 | Extremely Favorable | Strong deployment justified |
| 4 | Favorable | Above-average opportunity |
| 3 | Neutral | Selective approach required |
| 2 | Unfavorable | Reduce exposure |
| 1 | Extremely Unfavorable | Capital preservation |

### Level Weights

| Level | Focus | Composite Weight |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| L1 | Global Liquidity | 30% |
| L2 | Macroeconomics & Geopolitics | 25% |
| L3 | 4-Year Crypto Cycle & On-Chain | 25% |
| L4 | Sector Flows & Narratives | 20% |
| L5 | Protocol Technology | Per-project only |
| L6 | Project Fundamentals | Per-project only |

**Design rationale:** L1 gets highest weight — liquidity is the master variable. L5-L6 excluded from composite because they are project-specific. Composite (L1-L4) answers: 'Should I be in the market at all?' L5-L6 answer: 'Where specifically?'

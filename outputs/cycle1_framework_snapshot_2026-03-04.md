Global liquidity is at all-time highs — US M2 just hit $22.44 trillion in January 2026, its highest level ever recorded. ## LEVEL 1: GLOBAL LIQUIDITY — THE MASTER VARIABLE

### State: Structurally Expansive. Tactically Blocked.

US M2 money supply reached $22.44 trillion in January 2026 — an all-time high. Global M2 is trending toward $144 trillion. The Federal Reserve ended its Quantitative Tightening program in late 2025 and is now executing Reserve Management Purchases (RMPs), injecting base liquidity without the explicit signal of QE.

On paper, this is rocket fuel. In practice, the fuel is pooling at the base of the engine.

**Why isn't the liquidity flowing into risk assets?**
1. Oil prices are surging (US-Iran escalation)
2. Inflation fears are returning
3. The market is pricing *fewer* Fed rate cuts, not more
4. The transmission mechanism — low inflation + dovish Fed + risk appetite — is partially broken

This is what I call the **Liquidity Paradox of early 2026**: money supply at all-time high, risk assets in a bear market. It has happened before. It resolves when macro fear clears. The question is *when*, not *if*.

## LEVEL 2: MACROECONOMICS & GEOPOLITICS

### The Key Events Shaping This Moment

**1. US-Iran escalation (active, March 4, 2026)**
Bitcoin hit a 1-month high near $72,000 today — not because of bullish crypto fundamentals, but because of haven demand. Gold and BTC are both bid in the same flight. This validates Bitcoin's long-term digital gold narrative, but it is not a structural crypto bull signal. Haven pumps reverse when geopolitical fear fades.

**2. Oil surge → inflation risk → Fed constraints**
The transmission chain is clear: conflict → oil spike → inflation reacceleration → Fed cannot cut aggressively → liquidity stays constrained for risk assets → crypto under pressure.

**3. Dollar dynamics**
Complex environment. Haven demand pulls into USD, JPY, and gold simultaneously. If DXY breaks decisively above 105, expect additional compression on risk assets globally. Monitor closely.

**4. The regime we're in**
Post-easy-money. 2020–2021 was an anomaly. The normalization process (higher-for-longer rates relative to that era) continues to wash out leverage, compress speculation multiples, and punish assets with no fundamental floor. This is painful. It is also necessary. It is where the next cycle's strongest assets are separated from the noise.

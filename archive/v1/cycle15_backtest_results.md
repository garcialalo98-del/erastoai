## Risk Metrics Deep Dive

### Max Drawdown Comparison

| Strategy | Max DD | When | Recovery |
|----------|--------|------|----------|
| **Erasto** | **-16.5%** | 2022-Q2 (LUNA/3AC) | ~1 quarter |
| BTC B&H | -71.9% | 2022-Q4 (FTX bottom) | ~8 quarters |
| 60/40 | -46.9% | 2022-Q4 | ~6 quarters |

**Why Erasto survived 2022:** Score dropped to 2.23→Defensive (20% BTC, 0% alts, 80% stables) BEFORE the LUNA crash. With minimal exposure, the crash barely scratched the portfolio. BTC B&H holders watched $91K become $25K.

### The Model's Superpower: Bear Market Protection
Defensive mode during 2022 preserved capital. While BTC dropped from $45K to $16K (-64%), Erasto went from $59K to $51K (-14%). That dry powder fueled the 2023-2024 recovery.

### The Model's Weakness: Bull Market Lag
During explosive rallies (2020-Q4, 2021-Q1), Erasto underperforms. The scoring system reacts to confirmed conditions — by the time "Max Offense" triggers, BTC already made its first big move. This is a feature, not a bug: the model prioritizes not losing over maximum winning.

### The Real-World Tradeoff
- **Want max returns + can stomach -72% drawdowns?** → BTC buy-and-hold
- **Want 83% of returns with 23% of the pain?** → Erasto Model
- **Reality:** Most retail investors who try B&H sell the bottom → Erasto wins in practice

## Methodology

- **Scoring:** Quarterly L1-L4 composite scores applied retrospectively (Cycle 14 data)
- **No forward bias:** Each quarter uses PREVIOUS quarter's score for allocation
- **Alts proxy:** ETH (highest correlation to quality alts basket)
- **Stables yield:** 0% assumed (conservative — real yield would improve returns)
- **Rebalancing:** Quarterly at phase boundaries
- **Costs excluded:** ~0.1% per rebalance on major exchanges (negligible)
